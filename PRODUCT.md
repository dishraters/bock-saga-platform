# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves newcomers trying to understand Carl Borgen, Ior Bock, and the Bock Saga without getting lost in scattered pages, claims, names, and timelines. It also serves returning readers who want a searchable reference library for books, articles, interviews, terms, characters, and future AI-assisted explanations.

## Product Purpose

Build a modern, fast, expandable website for Carl Borgen and the Bock Saga. The site should turn the existing WordPress material into a clearer educational platform with structured learning paths, searchable content, a professional blog system, SEO foundations, and a future-ready "Ask the Bock Saga" assistant area.

Success means a first-time visitor can understand what the Bock Saga is, who Carl Borgen and Ior Bock are, how Root Language fits into the tradition, and where to keep reading. The site must also give Sam and Carl a clean technical base for future AI content workflows, articles, courses, resources, community features, and a knowledge-base assistant.

## Brand Personality

Serious, mythic, careful.

The tone should feel like a sober archive with atmosphere: curious, respectful of the source material, visually memorable, and clear about what comes from the Bock Saga tradition versus mainstream historical consensus.

## Anti-references

Do not make a generic wellness-mysticism landing page, conspiracy rabbit-hole site, academic database with no emotional pull, crypto/NFT-style hype page, or shallow AI template with vague "ancient wisdom" language.

Do not copy existing artwork from the current site unless permission exists. Use original imagery or abstract visual systems that suggest northern landscapes, old maps, stone, language, oral tradition, and mythic structure.

## Design Principles

1. Make the material legible before making it mysterious.
2. Treat Bock Saga claims as claims within the tradition, not as settled scholarly fact.
3. Give newcomers a guided path, then let deeper readers search and explore.
4. Build the first version like a future knowledge platform, not a brochure.
5. Use visual atmosphere to support learning, not to cover gaps in content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard navigation. Keep text readable on mobile. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Avoid meaning carried only by color, texture, or animation. Use clear language around contested claims so visitors can understand the distinction between tradition, memoir, interpretation, and historical scholarship.
